MicroAire Identity

Visual Identity Refresh

Client: MicroAire Surgical Instruments, LLC

MicroAire designs and manufactures powered orthopaedic and plastic surgery devices in Charlottesville, Virginia.

Goal: Update the brand's identity to better reflect the business and improve consistency visually between all of their products.

Team: Sarah Beth Martin: Design, Product Photography, Motion Graphics ● Elias Jones: Creative Services Lead ● VibeThink: Web Development Partner

The Color Scheme

MicroAire's brand needed to communicate a professional, credible and safe medical device manufacturer with fast and friendly customer service.

Their previous color schemes (red/black and blue/green) were not only outdated, but also no longer aligned with the company goals, including combining their segmented products (othopaedics and aesthetics) together under one umbrella.

The new color scheme represents stability, trust and cleanliness. Blues are often seen in operating rooms and throughout healthcare in hospitals and practices. Before the refresh, blue was already in use on the device hand pieces themselves for several years.

Cool gray tones with white space works with the blue throughout the identity for a softer and simpler look. The secondary pallet is provided to help emphasize specific items or areas and is especially useful in web design as visual cues to direct users.

“Color does not add a pleasant quality to design—it reinforces it.” — Pierre Bonnard
Before Select to Switch

Simplifying Logo Hierarchy

During the identity refresh process, the team worked to determine the value of the various individual product logos. After determining that the majority were inconsistent with the brand or were derivative of the corporate logo (which, in turn lessened its value), we found that it's best to remove the stylized logos entirely. This increases value of the corporate logo and places every product on the same level of the hierarchy.

Before Select to Switch

Website Redesign

MicroAire's website prior to the redesign was heavily focused on e-commerce sales ordering and tools for sales representatives. Unfortunately, this led to a complex and difficult navigation and wasn't complimentary of the company's brand. The outdated platform also made viewing the site difficult for any users on a mobile device. The team found it was necessary to not only redesign the online experience, but to also separate the sales experience from it's marketing initiatives.
